Interval Estimator Training

For techniques like Applied Information Economics, the range of various values needs to be estimated. To do so properly, the people that perform the estimation should have been "calibrated", that is trained in estimating their own uncertainty. On this website, you can train yourself as an estimator.

Tips for Estimation

  • Start with an unrealistically large interval. Then think about facts you know that help you shrink it.
  • Try to image a lottery in which you have a 1 in 10 chance to win. You could bet 100 Euro on this lottery or on your interval estimate being correct. Try to select an interval so that you are not sure whether you would prefer to lottery or the bet on your estimate. If you would for sure take your interval, it is too wide. If you would for sure take the lottery, the interval is too small.
